# Downloaded Papers

This directory contains research papers relevant to the study of LLMs and secret-keeping in story generation.

## Papers List

### 1. Creating Suspenseful Stories: Iterative Planning with Large Language Models
- **File**: `2402.17119_suspenseful_stories.pdf`
- **Authors**: Kaige Xie, Mark Riedl
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2402.17119](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.17119)
- **Venue**: EACL 2024
- **Why relevant**: First systematic work on suspenseful story generation with LLMs. Proposes iterative planning method grounded in psychological theory of suspense.

### 2. Modelling Suspense in Short Stories as Uncertainty Reduction over Neural Representation
- **File**: `2004.14905_modeling_suspense.pdf`
- **Authors**: David Wilmot, Frank Keller
- **Year**: 2020
- **arXiv**: [2004.14905](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.14905)
- **Venue**: ACL 2020
- **Why relevant**: Provides computational metrics for suspense (surprise and uncertainty reduction). Near human accuracy in predicting suspense.

### 3. Hierarchical Neural Story Generation
- **File**: `1805.04833_hierarchical_neural_story.pdf`
- **Authors**: Angela Fan, Mike Lewis, Yann Dauphin
- **Year**: 2018
- **arXiv**: [1805.04833](https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.04833)
- **Venue**: ACL 2018
- **Why relevant**: Foundational work on plan-then-write story generation. Introduces WritingPrompts dataset.

### 4. Generating Long-form Story Using Dynamic Hierarchical Outlining with Memory-Enhancement (DOME)
- **File**: `2412.13575_DOME_hierarchical_outlining.pdf`
- **Authors**: Qianyue Wang et al.
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2412.13575](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.13575)
- **Why relevant**: Addresses tension between fixed plans and adaptive generation. Shows that rigid outlines cause coherence issues.

### 5. Guiding and Diversifying LLM-Based Story Generation via Answer Set Programming
- **File**: `2406.00554_ASP_story_generation.pdf`
- **Authors**: Phoebe J. Wang, Max Kreminski
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2406.00554](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.00554)
- **Why relevant**: Demonstrates symbolic planning for story structure. Clean separation of planning and generation.

### 6. Navigating the Path of Writing: Outline-guided Text Generation
- **File**: `2404.13919_outline_guided_generation.pdf`
- **Authors**: Various
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2404.13919](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.13919)
- **Why relevant**: "Writing Path" method for outline-guided generation. Addresses consistency and organization in LLM writing.

### 7. StoryVerse: Towards Co-authoring Dynamic Plot with LLM-based Character Simulation
- **File**: `2405.13042_StoryVerse.pdf`
- **Authors**: Yi Wang, Qian Zhou, David Ledo
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2405.13042](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.13042)
- **Venue**: FDG 2024
- **Why relevant**: Narrative planning with LLM-based character simulation. Addresses author-defined plot points and dynamic story evolution.

### 8. Evaluating Creative Short Story Generation in Humans and Large Language Models
- **File**: `2411.02316_creative_short_story_eval.pdf`
- **Authors**: Mete Ismayilzada et al.
- **Year**: 2024
- **arXiv**: [2411.02316](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02316)
- **Why relevant**: Shows LLMs lack suspense and tension in generated stories. Provides creativity evaluation methodology.

### 9. Agents' Room: Narrative Generation through Multi-step Collaboration
- **File**: `agents_room_iclr2025.pdf`
- **Authors**: Fantine Huot, Reinald Kim Amplayo et al.
- **Year**: 2025
- **Venue**: ICLR 2025
- **Why relevant**: Multi-agent approach to story generation. Shows collaborative planning improves narrative quality.
